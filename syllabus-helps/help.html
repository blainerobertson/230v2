Getting Help

For this class there are multiple avenues of help, but keep in mind that you must have tried to solve the problem yourself prior to seeking help from others.

Your Peers - it is expected and encouraged for you to work together to help one another. Meet face-to-face, call one another, use virtual software (Skype, Google Hangouts, etc...).

The Discussion Boards - use the weekly discussion board to post and answer questions related to the topic of the week.

The Teaching Assistant - contact a teaching assistant to get help and answer your questions. The teaching assistants are shown below:

  • Andrew Jester (campus, online) — jes15007@byui.edu

The Professor — Make an appointment to visit at calendly.com/blainer or visit with me in class.

CIT Web Lab is located in STC 347. There you will find lab assistants who can help you.

CIT Web Lab — STC 347 is open for a limited number of hours each week with a TA available to help on a first come, first serve basis. The lab hours will be made available usually during the second week of the semester.

Tutoring Center — The BYU-Idaho tutoring center hires tutors to meet and assist students. Visit the tutoring center web site to check availability and schedule an appointment.
